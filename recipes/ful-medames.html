Egyptian Ful Medames (fava beans)

Description

Ful Medames, Egypt's iconic fava bean stew, is more than a dishâ€”it's a cherished tradition. This recipe shows you how to recreate its buttery, slow-simmered magic using a slow cooker. Bring the comforting aromas of Egyptian street food straight to your kitchen!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried fava beans
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped onions
  • Optional: chopped hard-boiled eggs for garnish
  • Optional: additional olive oil for drizzling

Steps

  1. Rinse the dried fava beans and soak them in water overnight.
  2. Drain and rinse the soaked beans.
  3. Place the beans in a slow cooker and cover with 4 cups of water.
  4. Add the baking soda, ground cumin, ground coriander, and salt to the slow cooker.
  5. Cook on low for 8-10 hours, or until the beans are tender.
  6. Once cooked, drain any excess water from the beans.
  7. In a bowl, mix the cooked beans with olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, chopped parsley, chopped tomatoes, and chopped onions.
  8. Serve warm, optionally garnished with chopped hard-boiled eggs and a drizzle of additional olive oil.
